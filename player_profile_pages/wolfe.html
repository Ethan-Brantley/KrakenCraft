Travis Wolfe

A cornerstone of justice and honesty in Jonasville, details are scarce regarding the early life of the enigmatic judge. His official town records are sealed and remain so except under express permission by the Sheriff.

Prior to accepting his present position, Wolfe worked as a private detective, building a remarkable reputation in neighboring settlements. His occupation took him across the badlands and beyond, never staying long in the same location. From missing brooches to missing persons, no case proved too small or too extensive for the wandering detective. Despite being willing to reminisce about old cases when prompted off-duty, he never says much about the final case that drove him away from that profession and nomadic way of life. He only responds with the cryptic response:

"Sometimes it feels like you couldn't possibly stand taller, like you're standing atop the world; Other times, let's just say it grabs you. And then it drops you. Then it drives you so far into the ground and buries you so deep, that you ain't never finding your way back up."
- Travis Wolfe

Some years after leaving behind his work as a private detective, a deadly virus ravaged through the Badlands, leaving many settlements devastated, including the one Wolfe would find himself isolated in for sixteen months. In the aftermath of the virus, Wolfe took a canteen filled with water, a horse, and a barrel of salt cod, and began traveling down the former spider web of trade routes, finding town after town of destruction and death. It was here, on horseback, that he first picked up radio chatter of a sanctuary for gathering survivors.

Jonasville

With much of his supplies depleted, Wolfe arrived in Jonasville, an old settlement town revitalized by a growing number of survivors in search of the radio broadcast. The town sheriff took notice of his experience as a private detective and the unique perspective it brought. Working closely with the Sheriff and helping to maintain peace during a time of heightened stress and chaos, he quickly gained the Sheriff's trust - remarkably quick, according to some residents. Not long after, the Sheriff offered Wolfe a position as the judge of the Jonasville courthouse, where he can be found most days.
